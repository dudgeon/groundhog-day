# Wiarton Non-Groundhog News Summary — Each Groundhog Day, 2022–2026

What else was happening in Wiarton, Ontario (South Bruce Peninsula) around each February 2nd?

---

## February 2, 2026

### Town Hall Construction Awarded
A nearly **$9.8 million contract** to convert the former Wiarton Foodland into a new town hall was awarded to Allan Hastings Limited, the lowest of eight bids. The project is expected to be completed by January 2027.
— [CKNXNewsToday](https://cknxnewstoday.ca/midwestern/news/2026/00/15/south-bruce-peninsula-town-hall-tender-awarded)

### Severe Winter Weather
Wiarton had already received nearly **350 cm of snow** by early February — almost a full winter's worth. A snow squall warning brought an additional 10–15 cm, with wind chills in the low minus-20s. Multiple Bruce County roads were closed and all school buses in Grey-Bruce and North Wellington were cancelled.
— [Instant Weather](https://instantweatherinc.com/article/ontario/2026/2/9/snowfall), [CKNXNewsToday](https://cknxnewstoday.ca/midwestern/news/2026/00/20/snowy-conditions-frigid-temps-persist-in-midwestern-ontario)

### Heritage Award Nominations Open
The Town of South Bruce Peninsula accepted nominations for its Heritage Award, with a deadline of February 4, 2026.
— [CKNXNewsToday](https://cknxnewstoday.ca/midwestern/news/2026/01/19/nominations-open-for-the-south-bruce-peninsula-heritage-award)

### Downtown Revitalization Funding
Council approved **$25,000** for downtown revitalization in Allenford, Hepworth, Sauble Beach, and Wiarton — covering facade improvements, signage, patios, and awnings — plus **$10,000** for a Business Accessibility Adaptability Grant.
— [South Bruce Peninsula](https://www.southbrucepeninsula.com/en/news/south-bruce-peninsula-collaborates-with-bruce-county-to-launch-the-spruce-the-bruce-program.aspx)

## February 2, 2025

### New Town Hall Design Presented to Council
Architect GM Diemert presented preliminary designs for the new Town Hall at **370 William Street** (the former grocery store site). The existing steel frame will be repurposed to save millions. Council chambers, staff areas, and updated exterior/landscaping were shown.
— [Bayshore Broadcasting](https://www.bayshorebroadcasting.ca/2025/02/04/council-to-see-some-new-town-hall-designs-in-wiarton/), [CKNXNewsToday](https://cknxnewstoday.ca/midwestern/news/2025/02/26/south-bruce-peninsula-looking-for-input-on-new-town-hall-space)

### Brutal Winter Continues — Road Closures and Snow Days
Grey and Bruce counties were pummeled by relentless snowstorms through January and February 2025. By late January, a significant weather event was declared with winds gusting up to **90 km/h**, whiteouts, and impassable roads. Approximately **25 county and provincial roads** north of Wiarton were closed over a three-day period, and some schools recorded up to **20 snow days**.
— [Bruce Peninsula Press](https://brucepeninsulapress.com/2025/02/04/whiteouts-impassable-roads-cause-road-and-school-closures/), [Bruce County](https://www.brucecounty.on.ca/news/2025-feb-18/update-motorists-advised-stay-closed-roads-february-18-2025)

### Mid-February Snow Squalls
Further snow squalls in mid-February dropped **15–25 cm** on the Bruce Peninsula and North Grey, with all county-managed roadways closed on February 18. School buses cancelled across Grey-Bruce, Huron-Perth, and Wellington County.
— [CKNXNewsToday](https://www.cknxnewstoday.ca/midwestern/news/2025/02/18/wintry-weather-continues-to-pummel-midwestern-ontario-closing-roads-and-schools)

## February 2, 2024

### Town Acquires Former Foodland for New Town Hall
South Bruce Peninsula announced it had entered into a binding agreement with Sobeys to purchase the former **Wiarton Foodland** site, to be converted into a new town hall and potential community services hub (Service Canada, Service Ontario, library). Using the existing steel frame was projected to reduce construction costs by up to **25%**.
— [CKNXNewsToday](https://cknxnewstoday.ca/midwestern/news/2024/02/07/south-bruce-peninsula-acquires-old-grocery-store-for-new-town-hall)

### Elm Street Infrastructure Work Wraps Up
Phase one of infrastructure work on Elm Street in Wiarton was completed, with the street reopened to traffic in early January after months of construction. Phase two (Elm Street from Berford to the town limit) was budgeted for later in 2024.
— [Bayshore Broadcasting](https://www.bayshorebroadcasting.ca/2024/01/04/elm-street-in-wiarton-opens-after-work-wraps-for-now/)

### Wiarton Transit Route Under Consideration
Bruce County was evaluating continued support for a transit route serving Wiarton through the end of the year.
— [Bayshore Broadcasting](https://www.bayshorebroadcasting.ca/2024/03/18/bruce-county-to-consider-supporting-transit-route-until-end-of-year/)

## February 2, 2023

### Bruce Peninsula Sportsmen's Association Family Day Event Returns
After a three-year hiatus due to COVID, the Bruce Peninsula Sportsmen's Association hosted its **Family Day event** on February 20, 2023, at the hatchery and clubhouse in Wiarton — a notable return of in-person community gatherings.
— [Bayshore Broadcasting](https://www.bayshorebroadcasting.ca/2023/02/18/bruce-peninsula/)

### Significant Weather Event Declared
Bruce County declared a **significant weather event** on February 22, 2023, due to observed and forecasted conditions affecting roads across the region.
— [Bruce County](https://www.brucecounty.on.ca/news/2023-feb-22/county-declares-significant-weather-event-february-22-2023)

### Boating Accident Criminal Negligence Case
Christopher Solomon, 42, pleaded guilty in Superior Court to causing the death of **Tyler Denning** by criminal negligence in a boating accident at Wiarton more than three years prior. The case was resolved in early 2023.
— [Blackburn News](https://blackburnnews.com/midwestern-ontario/midwestern-ontario-news/2023/02/02/groundhog-day-wiarton/)

### Budget Deliberations: Infrastructure on the Agenda
Council wrapped up draft 2023 budget deliberations targeting a sub-3% tax levy increase. Capital budget items included **Phase 2 of Elm Street** infrastructure, $657K for Silver Lake Road, and design work on Jewel Bridge and Park Head Bridge.
— [97.9 The Bruce](https://www.rockthebruce.ca/2023/01/10/south-bruce-peninsula-aims-for-less-than-three-per-cent-tax-increase/)

## February 2, 2022

### Community Hub Feasibility Study Underway
South Bruce Peninsula launched a **Community Hub Recreation Feasibility Study** to assess demand for a multi-use community hub on ~25 acres in north Wiarton. About 60 residents attended a virtual engagement session; an **indoor pool and walking track** were the most popular amenities. The final study report was delivered to council in March 2022.
— [Bayshore Broadcasting](https://www.bayshorebroadcasting.ca/2022/01/10/south-bruce-peninsula-is-starting-its-community-hub/), [South Bruce Peninsula OpenMic](https://openmicsouthbrucepeninsula.ca/recreation-feasibility-study)

### COVID Cancellations Continue
The 2022 Wiarton Willie Festival was cancelled due to the Omicron variant wave, and the prediction was **livestreamed only** — no public attendance. This extended the pandemic's multi-year disruption of Wiarton's normally bustling Groundhog Day crowds.
— [Blackburn News](https://blackburnnews.com/midwestern-ontario/2022/01/07/wiarton-willie-go-virtual-no-public-attendance-2022/)

### Rising Inflation Hits the Region
Canada's annual CPI increase reported in February 2022 was **5.7% year-over-year** — the highest since 1991. This economic pressure would later contribute to a labour lockout of South Bruce Peninsula unionized municipal workers in April 2022.
— [Bayshore Broadcasting](https://www.bayshorebroadcasting.ca/2022/04/05/locked-out-south-bruce-peninsula-unionized-workers/)
